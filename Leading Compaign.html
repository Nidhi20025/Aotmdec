Leading Compaign

The leading companies in the race to provide satellite internet in India and their "campaigns" (efforts/strategies) are Starlink, Reliance Jio, and Eutelsat OneWeb. All have received the necessary licenses from the Department of Telecommunications (DoT) and are in the final stages of regulatory approval before a full commercial launch.

Key Leading Compaign for Satellite Internet in India

Universal Connectivity

The primary objective and future possibility is to provide high-speed, reliable internet access to remote and difficult-to-reach regions (e.g., the Himalayas, islands, and rural villages) where terrestrial fiber optic cables are not economically viable. This will help achieve the goals of the government's Digital India and BharatNet initiatives.

Technological Shift

There is a significant transition from traditional Geostationary Orbit (GEO) satellites to advanced Low Earth Orbit (LEO) and Medium Earth Orbit (MEO) systems. LEO constellations, in particular, promise much lower latency, making satellite internet suitable for real-time applications like video calls and online education.

Private Sector Growth

The Indian government has opened the space sector to private participation with up to 100% Foreign Direct Investment (FDI), attracting global and domestic companies.

Diverse Applications

Beyond residential broadband, satellite internet is expected to enable critical applications across various sectors: E-governance & Telemedicine: Facilitating online government services and remote healthcare consultations in rural areas.

Policy and Regulatory Support

The Indian Space Policy 2023 provides a clear framework for private players, and the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) is actively shaping policies to facilitate the seamless rollout of services, including spectrum assignment recommendations.
