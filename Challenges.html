Challenges

Satellite internet deployment in India faces a combination of high costs, significant technical limitations (like weather interference,) and a complex regulatory environment. These challenges impact affordability, reliability, and national security considerations

Key Challenges for Satellite Internet in India

High Cost and Affordability

The primary barrier is the high cost of user terminals and monthly subscriptions, which are significantly more expensive than existing terrestrial broadband services in India.

Technical Limitations

Line of Sight and Weather: Satellite broadband requires a clear line of sight to the sky. This makes it vulnerable to service disruptions from physical obstructions.

Latency

While Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites significantly reduce latency compared to older Geostationary (GEO) satellites, some latency still exists, which can impact real-time, sensitive applications.

Infrastructure and Operational Issues

Ground Stations: Despite being space-based, satellite systems still require a substantial network of ground stations and local routers, which demands significant capital investment.

Market Dynamics

The industry faces concerns about monopolization, with a few global players dominating the LEO market. There is also intense competition and a dependency on imported technology, which can hinder the growth of indigenous satellite manufacturing in India.

Spectrum Allocation

Delays and disputes over the method of allocating spectrum (auction vs. administrative assignment) have slowed the market entry and rollout of services by major players like Starlink and OneWeb.
