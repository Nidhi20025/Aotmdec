What Satellite Internet Is

A satellite internet system uses communication satellites orbiting Earth to provide internet access, especially in remote or rural areas where traditional wired connections (like fiber or cable) are unavailable or impractical.

Why it is important for India.

Bridging the Digital Divide

With a large portion of the rural population lacking internet access, satellite internet is the only viable option for connectivity in remote areas like the Himalayan regions, islands (Andaman & Nicobar, Lakshadweep), and northeastern states. This ensures digital inclusion for all citizens, supporting the government's "Digital India" vision.

National Security and Strategic Autonomy

Satellite communication is essential for secure military communications and operational readiness in strategic border areas and isolated conflict zones. Developing indigenous satellite capabilities also helps reduce reliance on foreign-controlled systems, ensuring India's strategic autonomy and data sovereignty.

Disaster Management and Emergency Response

Terrestrial communication networks are often vulnerable to natural disasters like floods, earthquakes, and cyclones. Satellite internet provides a resilient and uninterrupted line of communication for early warning systems, real-time monitoring, and coordinating critical rescue and relief operations, which is vital for saving lives.

How it works.

Satellite(s)

Communication satellites orbiting the Earth act as relay stations in space. They receive signals from Earth, amplify them using a component called a transponder, and retransmit them back to Earth

Network Operations Center (NOC) / Gateway

A large ground station that serves as the bridge between the satellites and the terrestrial internet backbone (the high-speed network infrastructure that makes up the global internet).
